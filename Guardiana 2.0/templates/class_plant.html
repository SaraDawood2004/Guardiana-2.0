Plant

HERBS






Note : Herbs are plants with savory or aromatic properties. They are short-sized plants with soft, green, delicate stems without woody tissues. Most Herbs complete their life cycle within one or two seasons.

Examples : Basil, Coriander, Fennel, Rosemary, and Mint

SHRUBS






Note : Shrubs are medium-sized, woody plants taller than herbs but shorter than trees. They have bushy, hard, and woody stems with many branches.

Examples : Rose, Jasmine, Lemon, Tulsi, and Henna

TREES






Note : Trees are big and tall plants with an elongated stem or trunk.

Examples : Oak, Pine, and Banyan

CLIMBERS






Note : Climbers are plants that grow upwards by clinging to a support.
They often have specialized structures like tendrils, twining stems, or clinging roots that help them attach and climb.

Examples : Gourds, Jasmine, Cucumber, and Grapevine
