Grapevine



          




SCIENTIFIC NAME : Vitis vinifera

COMMON NAME : Grape

ORIGIN : Near East

SCIENTIFIC CLASSIFICATION:
  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Angiosperms
  • Order: Vitales
  • Family: Vitaceae
  • Genus: Vitis
CULTIVATION TIPS:
Sunlight :
1. Grapevines thrive in full sunlight.
2. They should receive at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight each day.
3. Adequate sunlight is essential for fruit development and ripening.
Watering :
1. Grapevines require regular watering, especially during the growing season.
2. Water deeply and infrequently to encourage deep root growth.
3. Ensure the soil is well-draining to prevent waterlogging and root diseases.
Temperature :
1. Grapevines grow best in temperatures between 60째F (15째C) and 85째F (29째C).
2. They can tolerate a range of temperatures but may suffer in extreme heat or cold.
3. In colder climates, protection is needed during winter to prevent frost damage.
Soil Type:
1. Grapevines prefer well-draining, loamy soil.
2. A slightly acidic to neutral pH range of 6.0 to 7.5 is ideal.
3. Soil should be enriched with organic matter, such as compost, to improve fertility.
4. Good drainage is crucial to prevent root rot and other soil-borne diseases.

USES:
  • Food: Consumed fresh, dried as raisins, or processed into juice and wine.
  • Health: Grapes are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, offering various health benefits.
  • Culinary: Used in cooking and baking, enhancing the flavor of dishes and desserts.
  • Skincare: Grape seed extract is used in skincare products for its moisturizing and antioxidant properties.
  • Industrial: Grape by-products are used in making vinegar, oil, and other products.
