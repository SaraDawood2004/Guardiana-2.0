Peanut Leaf: Early Leaf Spot





Peanut Leaf

Peanut leaves are compound leaves, typically with four leaflets arranged oppositely. Each leaflet is oval-shaped and has a pointed tip. The leaves are bright green in color and can grow up to several inches in length.

Preventive Measures for Early Leaf Spot :

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate peanut crops with non-host plants to reduce the buildup of pathogens in the soil.
  • Plant Resistant Varieties: Choose peanut varieties that are resistant to Early Leaf Spot.
  • Proper Spacing: Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation and reduce humidity around the plants.
  • Weed Control: Keep the area around the peanut plants free from weeds to prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Sanitation: Remove and destroy any infected plant debris promptly to prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Irrigation Management: Avoid overhead irrigation and use drip irrigation to reduce humidity and prevent fungal growth.
